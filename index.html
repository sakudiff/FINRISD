Section 11: Strategic Risk Recommendations

Based on the quantitative analysis of USD/PHP data (2019-2026), these four steps will improve the firm’s risk posture:

1. Ditch VaR for CVaR

Value at Risk (VaR) is a flawed metric. It is not “coherent” because it fails the test of subadditivity: it can wrongly suggest that a diversified portfolio is riskier than the sum of its parts. The institution should switch to Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR). Unlike VaR, which ignores the “tail,” CVaR accounts for the worst-case scenarios and behaves logically when assets are combined.

2. Trade Dollars for Pesos

To cap daily losses at PHP 20,000, move capital out of dollars and into PHP cash or T-bills. This exploits the Translation Invariance of coherent risk measures: moving assets into the base currency reduces total risk \(\rho(X)\) by exactly the amount moved. It is a mathematical certainty, not a forecast.

3. Face the Price of Safety

Risk management is never a free lunch. Capping one-day losses at PHP 20,000 requires gutting the dollar position so severely that it eliminates potential gains. This safety comes at an opportunity cost of approximately PHP 5.4m in 99th-percentile upside. Managers must decide if avoiding a small daily loss is worth sacrificing millions in potential profit.
